# **Case Study: Punjab Soil Degradation due to Intensive Farming**

---

### **Background**

Punjab, known as one of India’s major agricultural states, played a key role during the Green Revolution. To increase crop production, farmers began using large amounts of chemical fertilizers, pesticides, and high-yield crop varieties.

While this approach initially increased productivity, continuous and excessive use of chemicals over the years led to serious degradation of soil quality.

---

### **Environmental Issue**

* Soil pollution due to excessive use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides
* Unsustainable agricultural practices
* Decline in soil health and nutrient balance
* Lack of awareness about long-term environmental impact

---

### **Impact**

#### **On Soil**

* Reduction in soil fertility
* Loss of essential nutrients
* Decrease in soil productivity over time

#### **On Environment**

* Contamination of groundwater through chemical seepage
* Reduction in biodiversity (microorganisms in soil)

#### **On Agriculture**

* Increased dependence on chemical inputs
* Declining crop yield in the long term
* Higher cost of farming

---

### **Key Learning**

* Excessive use of chemicals leads to long-term soil degradation
* Sustainable agricultural practices are necessary to maintain soil health
* Organic farming and crop rotation help restore soil fertility
* Soil conservation is essential for food security
* Awareness and education are important for responsible resource use

---

This case strongly supports:

* Soil pollution
* Causes and prevention
