# **Case Study: Ganga River Industrial Pollution**

---

### **Background**

The Ganga River is one of the most important rivers in India, supporting millions of people for drinking water, agriculture, and daily activities. However, over the years, untreated industrial waste, sewage, and chemical effluents from factories have been discharged into the river.

Industries such as tanneries, chemical plants, and textile units have contributed significantly to water pollution in certain stretches of the river.

---

### **Environmental Issue**

* Water pollution due to discharge of untreated industrial waste
* Contamination of water bodies with toxic chemicals and heavy metals
* Bioaccumulation of pollutants in aquatic organisms
* Lack of effective waste treatment and monitoring systems

---

### **Impact**

#### **On Human Health**

* Spread of waterborne diseases
* Exposure to toxic substances through contaminated water and food
* Long-term health issues in communities dependent on river water

---

#### **On Aquatic Life**

* Death of fish and other aquatic organisms
* Reduction in biodiversity
* Disturbance in aquatic ecosystems

---

#### **On Environment**

* Severe degradation of water quality
* Pollution spreading through the food chain
* Long-term contamination of river ecosystem

---

### **Key Learning**

* Industrial waste must be treated before being released into water bodies
* Water pollution can directly affect human health through the food chain
* Toxic substances accumulate over time and cause long-term damage
* Strong monitoring and enforcement of environmental laws are necessary
* Sustainable industrial practices are essential for protecting water resources

---
