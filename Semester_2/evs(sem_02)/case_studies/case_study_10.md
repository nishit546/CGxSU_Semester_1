# **Case Study: Goa Mining Belt**

---

### **Background**

Goa has been one of India’s प्रमुख regions for iron ore mining. Over the years, extensive mining activities were carried out to meet industrial and export demands. However, unregulated and excessive mining led to serious environmental concerns.

Due to these issues, mining operations were temporarily banned by the Supreme Court of India to control environmental damage and enforce regulations.

---

### **Environmental Issue**

* Overextraction of mineral resources
* Unsustainable mining practices
* Lack of proper environmental management
* Degradation of land and surrounding ecosystems

---

### **Impact**

#### **On Land and Soil**

* Land degradation and loss of fertile soil
* Formation of pits and abandoned mining sites
* Increased soil erosion

#### **On Water Resources**

* Contamination of nearby rivers and groundwater
* Increased sedimentation in water bodies

#### **On Environment**

* Loss of vegetation and biodiversity
* Air pollution due to dust from mining operations

#### **On Society**

* Impact on local communities and livelihoods
* Conflict between economic benefits and environmental protection

---

### **Key Learning**

* Mineral resources must be used in a controlled and sustainable manner
* Mining activities require strict environmental regulations
* Environmental impact assessment is essential before resource extraction
* Overexploitation leads to long-term environmental damage
* Sustainable practices are necessary to balance development and conservation
