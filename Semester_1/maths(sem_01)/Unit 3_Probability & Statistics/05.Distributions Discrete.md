

# 🎲 **Discrete Probability Distributions**

A **discrete probability distribution** provides the probabilities of **countable outcomes** of a random variable — such as the number of successes, failures, or arrivals in a process. The key property is that
$$[
\sum P(X = x_i) = 1
]$$
Discrete distributions are essential for understanding real-world events that involve **counting** — like the number of students passing an exam, emails received per hour, or defective items in a batch.

There are three main discrete distributions:
1️⃣ **Bernoulli Distribution**
2️⃣ **Binomial Distribution**
3️⃣ **Poisson Distribution**

---

## 🪙 **1️⃣ Bernoulli Distribution**

### 📘 **Definition:**

The **Bernoulli Distribution** is the simplest form of a probability distribution. It represents a **single trial** that has **two possible outcomes** — typically called **success (1)** and **failure (0)**.

* Probability of success = **p**
* Probability of failure = **q = 1 - p**

It’s used in binary situations — such as pass/fail, yes/no, or win/lose experiments.

📘 **Formula:**
$$[
P(X = x) = p^x(1 - p)^{1 - x}, \quad x \in {0,1}
]$$

📊 **Mean (E[X]) = p**
📊 **Variance (Var[X]) = p(1 - p)**

---

### 🎨 **Visual Representation**

```
Bernoulli Distribution (p = 0.6)
 Probability
    │
1.0 │         █
0.8 │         █
0.6 │         █
0.4 │         █        █
0.2 │         █        █
0.0 ├──────────────────────────
      0 (Failure)   1 (Success)
```

---

### 💡 **Examples with Detailed Explanation**

1️⃣ **🪙 Coin Toss**

* p(Head) = 0.5, p(Tail) = 0.5
  ✅ **P(X=1)=0.5** → Equal chance for head or tail.

2️⃣ **🏭 Product Test (Pass/Fail)**

* p(Pass) = 0.9
  ✅ 9 out of 10 products are expected to pass quality control.

3️⃣ **💡 Bulb Working or Not**

* p(Working) = 0.98
  ✅ Only 2% failure rate → Highly reliable bulbs.

4️⃣ **🌐 Server Request Success**

* p(Success) = 0.95
  ✅ 95% chance that a request completes successfully.

5️⃣ **🛒 Customer Purchase**

* p(Buy) = 0.3
  ✅ Out of 100 customers, about 30 will make a purchase.

6️⃣ **🎓 Exam Result (Pass/Fail)**

* p(Pass) = 0.8
  ✅ 80% probability of a student passing an exam.

## 🎯 **2️⃣ Binomial Distribution**

### 📘 **Definition:**

The **Binomial Distribution** extends the Bernoulli distribution to **multiple independent trials**. It gives the probability of getting exactly **k successes** in **n independent experiments**, each with the same success probability **p**.

📘 **Formula:**
$$[
P(X = k) = \binom{n}{k}p^k(1 - p)^{n - k}
]$$

where

* ( n ) = number of trials
* ( k ) = number of successes
* ( p ) = probability of success

📊 **Mean (E[X]) = np**
📊 **Variance (Var[X]) = np(1 - p)**

---

### 🎨 **Visual Representation**

```
Binomial Distribution (n=5, p=0.5)
 Probability
    │
0.3 │        ●
0.2 │     ● ● ●
0.1 │  ● ● ● ● ●
0.0 ├─────────────────────
     0  1  2  3  4  5
         Number of successes
```

---

### 💡 **Examples with Detailed Explanation**

1️⃣ **🪙 Coin Toss (n=3, p=0.5)**
$$[
P(X=2) = \binom{3}{2}(0.5)^3 = 0.375
]$$
✅ Probability of getting exactly 2 heads = **37.5%**.

2️⃣ **🏭 Product Testing (n=5, p=0.8)**
$$[
P(X=5) = (0.8)^5 = 0.32768
]$$
✅ 32.7% chance that all 5 products pass.

3️⃣ **🛍️ Customer Purchases (n=10, p=0.3)**
$$[
E[X] = np = 10 × 0.3 = 3
]$$
✅ On average, 3 customers will buy.

4️⃣ **⚙️ Defective Items (n=20, p=0.1)**
$$[
Var(X) = np(1-p) = 1.8
]$$
✅ Low variation → consistent production quality.

5️⃣ **🎓 Students Passing (n=6, p=0.7)**
$$[
E[X] = 4.2
]$$
✅ About 4 students expected to pass out of 6.

6️⃣ **📞 Sales Calls (n=8, p=0.25)**
$$[
P(X ≥ 3) = 0.31
]$$
✅ 31% chance of making 3 or more successful calls.

## 📞 **3️⃣ Poisson Distribution**

### 📘 **Definition:**

The **Poisson Distribution** models the **number of times an event occurs** in a fixed time or space interval, given a known **average rate (λ)**.
It is suitable for **rare or random events** happening independently — such as phone calls, defects, or arrivals.

📘 **Formula:**
$$[
P(X = k) = \frac{e^{-λ} λ^k}{k!}, \quad k = 0,1,2,...
]$$

📊 **Mean (E[X]) = λ**
📊 **Variance (Var[X]) = λ**

---

### 🎨 **Visual Representation**

```
Poisson Distribution (λ=3)
 Probability
    │
0.3 │          ●
0.2 │       ● ●
0.1 │    ● ●
0.0 ├──────────────────────────
      0  1  2  3  4  5  6
           Number of events
```

---

### 💡 **Examples with Detailed Explanation**

1️⃣ **📞 Calls Received per Hour (λ=5)**
$$[
P(X=3) = e^{-5} × \frac{5^3}{3!} = 0.140
]$$
✅ 14% chance of receiving exactly 3 calls.

2️⃣ **📧 Emails per Minute (λ=2)**
$$[
P(X=0) = e^{-2} = 0.135
]$$
✅ 13.5% chance of receiving no emails.

3️⃣ **🚗 Cars at a Toll Booth (λ=10)**
$$[
E[X] = λ = 10
]$$
✅ On average, 10 cars pass per hour.

4️⃣ **💡 Defective Bulbs (λ=1)**
$$[
P(X ≤ 2) = e^{-1}(1 + 1 + 0.5) = 0.918
]$$
✅ 91.8% chance of ≤2 defective bulbs per batch.

5️⃣ **🌧️ Raindrops per Minute (λ=4)**
$$[
P(X>2)=1-(P(0)+P(1)+P(2))=0.761
]$$
✅ 76.1% chance of more than 2 raindrops.

6️⃣ **🏥 Patients Arriving (λ=3)**
$$[
P(X=5)=e^{-3}×\frac{3^5}{120}=0.101
]$$
✅ 10% chance of exactly 5 patients in an hour.
