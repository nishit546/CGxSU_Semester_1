# **Case Study: Project Tiger**

---

### **Background**

Project Tiger was launched in India in 1973 to address the rapid decline in the population of tigers due to hunting, habitat destruction, and human interference. The initiative aimed to protect tigers by conserving their natural habitats and establishing protected areas known as tiger reserves.

Over time, the project expanded across multiple states, becoming one of the most successful wildlife conservation programs.

---

### **Environmental Issue**

* Loss of biodiversity due to human activities
* Decline in species population caused by habitat destruction and hunting
* Imbalance in ecosystem due to disappearance of top predators
* Lack of effective conservation measures

---

### **Impact**

#### **On Biodiversity**

* Significant increase in tiger population over time
* Protection of other species sharing the same habitat
* Preservation of genetic diversity

#### **On Ecosystem**

* Restoration of ecological balance
* Stabilization of food chains and trophic levels
* Improvement in forest ecosystem health

#### **On Conservation Awareness**

* Increased awareness about wildlife conservation
* Strengthening of environmental policies and protected areas

---

### **Key Learning**

* Conservation efforts can successfully restore endangered species
* Protecting one species helps in conserving the entire ecosystem
* Biodiversity conservation requires long-term planning and strict protection measures
* In-situ conservation (within natural habitat) is highly effective
* Government initiatives play a crucial role in environmental protection
