# **Unit 2**

## **Food Chain, Food Web, and Ecological Pyramids**

Understanding how energy moves through an ecosystem is essential for analyzing ecological balance. This movement of energy and nutrients occurs through structured feeding relationships known as **food chains**, **food webs**, and **ecological pyramids**.

---

## **1. Food Chain**

A food chain is a **linear sequence of organisms** where each organism is eaten by the next one.

### **Definition**

> A food chain represents the transfer of energy from one organism to another through feeding relationships.

---

### **Basic Structure of a Food Chain**

A typical food chain consists of:

* **Producers → Primary Consumers → Secondary Consumers → Tertiary Consumers**

---

### **Example of a Food Chain**

Grass → Deer → Tiger

* Grass (Producer) makes food using sunlight
* Deer (Herbivore) eats grass
* Tiger (Carnivore) eats deer

---

### **Types of Food Chains**

#### **1. Grazing Food Chain**

* Starts with green plants
* Example: Grass → Goat → Human

#### **2. Detritus Food Chain**

* Starts with dead organic matter
* Example: Dead leaves → Earthworms → Birds

---

### **Limitations of Food Chain**

* It is too simple and does not represent real ecosystems
* One organism may have multiple food sources
* It does not show interconnected relationships

---

## **2. Food Web**

A food web is a **complex network of interconnected food chains**.

### **Definition**

> A food web shows multiple feeding relationships within an ecosystem.

---

### **Why Food Web is Important**

* More realistic representation of nature
* Provides stability to ecosystem
* If one species is removed, others can survive

---

### **Example of Food Web**

In a grassland ecosystem:

* Grass is eaten by deer, rabbits, and insects
* Rabbits are eaten by foxes
* Insects are eaten by birds
* Foxes and birds may be eaten by larger predators

This creates multiple interconnected paths of energy flow.

---

### **Comparison: Food Chain vs Food Web**

| Feature   | Food Chain     | Food Web       |
| --------- | -------------- | -------------- |
| Structure | Simple         | Complex        |
| Pathway   | Single         | Multiple       |
| Stability | Less stable    | More stable    |
| Realism   | Less realistic | More realistic |

---

## **3. Ecological Pyramids**

Ecological pyramids represent the **distribution of energy, biomass, or number of organisms** at different trophic levels.

---

### **Types of Ecological Pyramids**

---

### **1. Pyramid of Numbers**

* Shows the number of organisms at each level

**Example:**

* Grass (many) → Rabbits (less) → Foxes (few)

---

### **2. Pyramid of Biomass**

* Shows total mass of living organisms at each level

**Key Point:**

* Indicates the quantity of living matter

---

### **3. Pyramid of Energy**

* Shows energy flow at each trophic level

**Important Features:**

* Always upright
* Energy decreases at each level

---

## **4. Concept of Trophic Levels**

Each step in a food chain or web is called a trophic level.

* Level 1 → Producers
* Level 2 → Herbivores
* Level 3 → Carnivores
* Level 4 → Top predators

---

## **5. Energy Loss in Ecosystem (10% Law)**

One of the most important concepts:

> Only about **10% of energy** is transferred from one trophic level to the next.

### **Implication:**

* Energy decreases as we move up the food chain
* This limits the number of trophic levels

---

### **Example**

If plants have 1000 units of energy:

* Herbivores → 100 units
* Carnivores → 10 units

---

## **6. Importance of Food Chain, Web, and Pyramids**

* Help in understanding energy flow
* Explain ecological balance
* Show interdependence of organisms
* Useful in environmental management

---

## **7. Impact of Disturbance**

If one organism is removed:

* Food chain gets disturbed
* Food web becomes unstable
* Ecosystem balance is affected

Example:

* Removal of predators → increase in herbivores → overgrazing

---

## **Conclusion**

Food chains, food webs, and ecological pyramids are essential tools for understanding how ecosystems function. They explain how energy flows, how organisms are interconnected, and why maintaining balance in nature is critical.
