# **Case Study: Delhi Air Pollution Crisis**

---

### **Background**

Delhi has experienced repeated episodes of severe air pollution, especially during winter months. Factors such as vehicle emissions, industrial activities, construction dust, and seasonal crop residue burning in nearby states combine with weather conditions like low wind speed and temperature inversion.

These conditions trap pollutants close to the ground, leading to the formation of dense smog that persists for several days.

---

### **Environmental Issue**

* Severe air pollution due to burning of fossil fuels and biomass
* High concentration of pollutants such as particulate matter (PM2.5, PM10), nitrogen oxides, and sulfur dioxide
* Formation of smog due to atmospheric conditions
* Inadequate control measures and enforcement

---

### **Impact**

#### **On Human Health**

* Increase in respiratory diseases such as asthma and bronchitis
* Eye irritation and breathing difficulties
* Long-term cardiovascular and lung-related problems

---

#### **On Environment**

* Significant reduction in air quality
* Poor visibility affecting transportation
* Damage to plants and urban ecosystems

---

#### **On Society**

* Disruption of daily activities
* Closure of schools and restrictions on outdoor movement
* Increased pressure on healthcare systems
* Government interventions such as traffic restrictions

---

### **Key Learning**

* Air pollution can have immediate and severe impacts on human health
* Rapid urbanization and fossil fuel use increase environmental risks
* Effective pollution control measures are essential in cities
* Continuous monitoring and strict regulations are necessary
* Understanding atmospheric conditions is important for managing pollution
