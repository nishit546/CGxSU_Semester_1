# **Case Study: Sariska Tiger Reserve Tiger Reintroduction**

---

### **Background**

Sariska Tiger Reserve in Rajasthan once had a healthy population of tigers, which acted as top predators in the ecosystem. However, due to poaching and human activities, the tiger population declined drastically and eventually became locally extinct by the early 2000s.

To restore ecological balance, tigers were reintroduced into the reserve starting in 2008 through a planned conservation program.

---

### **Environmental Issue**

* Disruption of ecosystem due to loss of a top predator
* Imbalance in trophic levels
* Overpopulation of herbivores such as deer and other grazing animals
* Lack of control over food chain dynamics

---

### **Impact**

#### **Before Reintroduction**

* Increase in herbivore population
* Overgrazing of vegetation
* Decline in forest regeneration
* Disturbance in habitat structure

---

#### **After Reintroduction**

* Control over herbivore population
* Gradual recovery of vegetation
* Improvement in biodiversity
* Restoration of ecological balance

---

### **Key Learning**

* Top predators play a crucial role in maintaining ecosystem stability
* Removal of predators leads to imbalance in food chains
* Reintroduction of species can help restore ecosystem functioning
* Proper wildlife management is essential for conservation
* Ecosystems depend on balanced trophic interactions

---

### **Supports Concepts**

* Ecosystem structure
* Trophic levels
* Food chain balance
* Role of consumers (predators)
