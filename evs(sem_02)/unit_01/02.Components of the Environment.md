# **Unit 1**

## **Components of the Environment and Earth’s Major Spheres**

The environment refers to everything that surrounds us, including both living and non-living elements. Understanding its structure is essential for studying how natural systems function and how human activities impact them.

To simplify this understanding, the environment is classified into **components** and **major spheres**.

---

## **Components of the Environment**

The environment is broadly divided into two types of components:

### **1. Biotic Components (Living Elements)**

Biotic components include all living organisms present in the environment, such as:

* Plants
* Animals
* Humans
* Microorganisms

These components are interdependent and form complex relationships, such as food chains and ecosystems.

---

### **2. Abiotic Components (Non-Living Elements)**

Abiotic components are the physical and chemical elements of the environment. These include:

* Air
* Water
* Soil
* Sunlight
* Temperature

They provide the necessary conditions for the survival and growth of living organisms.

---

## **Major Spheres of the Environment**

The environment is further divided into four major spheres, each representing a specific aspect of the Earth system.

---

### **1. Atmosphere (Air Layer)**

The atmosphere is the layer of gases surrounding the Earth.

**Key features:**

* Contains oxygen required for respiration
* Plays a major role in weather and climate

**Importance:**

* Protects life from harmful solar radiation
* Regulates temperature
* Supports life processes

---

### **2. Hydrosphere (Water Resources)**

The hydrosphere includes all forms of water on Earth, such as:

* Oceans
* Rivers
* Lakes
* Groundwater
* Glaciers

**Importance:**

* Essential for all living organisms
* Supports agriculture and industry
* Maintains ecological balance

---

### **3. Lithosphere (Land Surface)**

The lithosphere refers to the Earth’s solid outer layer, including:

* Soil
* Rocks
* Minerals

**Importance:**

* Provides resources such as minerals and fossil fuels
* Supports agriculture
* Serves as a habitat for many organisms

---

### **4. Biosphere (Zone of Life)**

The biosphere is the region of the Earth where life exists. It includes all living organisms and their interactions with other spheres.

**Importance:**

* Maintains ecological balance
* Supports biodiversity
* Connects all other spheres

---

## **Interconnection Between the Spheres**

All four spheres are interconnected and depend on each other.

For example:

* Plants require air (atmosphere), water (hydrosphere), and soil (lithosphere)
* Animals depend on plants and environmental conditions for survival

A disturbance in one sphere can directly or indirectly affect the others.
