# **Case Study: Chipko Movement**

---

### **Background**

The Chipko Movement began in the 1970s in the Himalayan region of Uttarakhand, India. Local villagers, especially women, protested against commercial logging of forests by hugging trees to prevent them from being cut down.

The movement emerged as a response to large-scale deforestation by contractors, which was affecting the livelihood of local communities and disturbing the natural environment.

---

### **Environmental Issue**

* Overexploitation of forest resources
* Deforestation due to commercial activities
* Lack of sustainable use of natural resources
* Ignoring the dependence of local communities on forests

---

### **Impact**

#### **On Forest Resources**

* Reduction in forest cover
* Loss of biodiversity
* Increased soil erosion

#### **On Local Environment**

* Increased risk of landslides and floods
* Disturbance in water availability
* Degradation of land quality

#### **Outcome of the Movement**

* Ban on tree cutting in certain regions
* Increased awareness about forest conservation
* Recognition of community participation in resource management

---

### **Key Learning**

* Forest resources must be used sustainably
* Local communities play a crucial role in conservation
* Overexploitation leads to long-term environmental damage
* Conservation efforts can successfully protect natural resources
* Sustainable development requires balancing human needs and environmental protection

---

This case strongly supports:

* Forest resources and their importance
* Conservation of natural resources
* Sustainable use
