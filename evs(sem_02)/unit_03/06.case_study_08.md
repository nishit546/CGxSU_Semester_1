# **Case Study: Chennai Water Crisis**

---

### **Background**

In 2019, Chennai, one of India’s major metropolitan cities, faced a severe water crisis. The city’s main reservoirs dried up due to insufficient rainfall, overdependence on groundwater, and poor water management practices.

Rapid urbanization and population growth further increased the demand for water, making the situation critical.

---

### **Environmental Issue**

* Overexploitation of water resources, especially groundwater
* Lack of sustainable water management practices
* Failure to conserve and store rainwater
* Imbalance between water demand and supply

---

### **Impact**

#### **On Daily Life**

* Shortage of drinking water
* Dependence on water tankers
* Disruption of normal activities

#### **On Environment**

* Depletion of groundwater levels
* Drying of lakes and reservoirs
* Reduced water availability for ecosystems

#### **On Economy**

* Increased cost of water supply
* Impact on industries and businesses
* Pressure on public infrastructure

---

### **Key Learning**

* Water is a limited and critical natural resource
* Overuse of groundwater leads to long-term scarcity
* Rainwater harvesting and conservation are essential
* Urban planning must include sustainable water management
* Efficient use of resources is necessary for sustainability

---

This case strongly supports:

* Water resources and conservation
* Sustainable use of natural resources
