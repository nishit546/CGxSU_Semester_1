# Solved examples (step-by-step)

**Q1.** A die is rolled. If the outcome is an even number, what is the probability that it is a prime number?
**Solution (step-by-step):**

1. Sample space for a die: ({1,2,3,4,5,6}).
2. Given event (B): outcome is even (={2,4,6}). So (P(B)=3/6).
3. Event (A): outcome is prime (={2,3,5}).
4. Intersection (A\cap B={2}) so (P(A\cap B)=1/6).
5. Use formula (P(A|B)=\dfrac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}=\dfrac{1/6}{3/6}=\dfrac{1}{3}.)

**Answer:** (\boxed{1/3})

---

**Q2.** A die is thrown twice and the sum observed is 6. What is the conditional probability that the number 4 has appeared at least once?
**Solution (step-by-step):**

1. List ordered pairs (first,second) with sum 6: ((1,5),(2,4),(3,3),(4,2),(5,1)). So there are 5 equally likely outcomes given the sum 6.
2. Outcomes with at least one 4: ((2,4),(4,2)) â€” 2 outcomes.
3. Conditional probability = number favorable / total = (2/5).

**Answer:** (\boxed{2/5})

---

**Q3.** A coin is tossed three times. Find (P(E|F)) for the following:

**(i)** (E): Head on third toss. (F): Heads on first two tosses.
**Solution:** Given (F), first two tosses are (H,H). Third toss can be (H) or (T). So sample space under (F) = ({HHH, HHT}). (E) occurs only in ({HHH}). Thus (P(E|F)=1/2).
**Answer:** (\boxed{1/2})

---

**(ii)** (E): At least two heads. (F): At most two heads.
**Solution (step-by-step):**

1. Total outcomes for 3 tosses = 8.
2. â€œAt least two headsâ€ = outcomes with exactly 2 heads or 3 heads. Exactly 2 heads = 3 outcomes. Exactly 3 heads = 1 outcome â†’ at least two heads = (3+1=4) outcomes.
3. â€œAt most two headsâ€ = all outcomes except the one with 3 heads â†’ (8-1=7) outcomes.
4. Intersection (E\cap F) = outcomes with exactly 2 heads (because 3 heads is not in (F)) = 3 outcomes.
5. (P(E|F)=\dfrac{#(E\cap F)}{#(F)}=\dfrac{3}{7}.)

**Answer:** (\boxed{3/7})

---

**(iii)** (E): At most two tails. (F): At least one tail.
**Solution (step-by-step):**

1. Total outcomes = 8.
2. â€œAt most two tailsâ€ = all outcomes except (TTT) â†’ (8-1=7) outcomes.
3. â€œAt least one tailâ€ = all outcomes except (HHH) â†’ (8-1=7) outcomes.
4. Intersection excludes both (HHH) and (TTT) â†’ (8-2=6) outcomes.
5. (P(E|F)=\dfrac{6/8}{7/8}=\dfrac{6}{7}.)

**Answer:** (\boxed{6/7})

---

**Q4.** A card is drawn from a well-shuffled deck of 52 cards. If the selected card is a face card (Jack, Queen, King), what is the probability that it is a King?
**Solution (step-by-step):**

1. Face cards = J, Q, K of each suit â†’ (4+4+4=12) cards.
2. Kings among face cards = 4.
3. (P(\text{King} \mid \text{Face}) = \dfrac{4}{12}=\dfrac{1}{3}.)

**Answer:** (\boxed{1/3})

---

**Q5.** Two dice are rolled and the sum obtained is 7. What is the conditional probability that one of the dice shows 3?
**Solution (step-by-step):**

1. Ordered outcomes with sum 7: ((1,6),(2,5),(3,4),(4,3),(5,2),(6,1)) â€” 6 equally likely outcomes.
2. Outcomes where one die is 3: ((3,4),(4,3)) â€” 2 outcomes.
3. Conditional probability (=2/6=1/3.)

**Answer:** (\boxed{1/3})
